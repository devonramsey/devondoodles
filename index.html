The Ramsey Test Kitchen Vol. I No. 001 — Cinnamon & Sugar Est. 2026

Devondoodles.

A house take on the snickerdoodle — pillowy centers, a brittle cinnamon-sugar shell, and the faint tang of cream of tartar that keeps every batch honest.

Yield
48 cookies
Bake
10 minutes
Oven
400° F
Keeps
7 days, sealed
Mise en Place

The pantry.

For the dough

  • All-purpose flour sifted 330 g · 2¾ c
  • Baking soda 6 g · 1 tsp
  • Cream of tartar 6 g · 2 tsp
  • Fine salt 1.5 g · ¼ tsp
  • Unsalted butter 1 stick, softened 113 g · ½ c
  • Vegetable shortening solid 95 g · ½ c
  • Granulated sugar 300 g · 1½ c
  • Eggs large 2

For the rolling sugar

  • Granulated sugar 50 g · ¼ c
  • Ground cinnamon 12 g · 1½ tbsp
Method

Nine moves, start to finish.

  1. Heat the oven.

    Preheat to 400°F (200°C) with one rack in the top third and one in the bottom third — you'll rotate halfway.

  2. Sift the dry.

    Sift together the flour, baking soda, cream of tartar, and salt. Set aside. The cream of tartar is the whole point — don't skip it.

  3. Cream butter, shortening, sugar.

    With an electric mixer on medium, beat the butter, shortening, and sugar until light and fluffy . Scrape the bowl down once it looks unified.

  4. Eggs in.

    Add both eggs and beat to combine. The mixture should look glossy and pale, like buttercream.

  5. Bring in the dry.

    Add the sifted dry ingredients and beat until just combined. Stop the moment streaks of flour disappear.

  6. Mix the cinnamon sugar.

    Whisk the rolling sugar and cinnamon together in a small bowl until uniformly rust-colored.

  7. Form & coat.

    Roll the dough into ~20 g balls, tumble each through the cinnamon sugar, and arrange about 2 inches apart on lined sheet pans.

  8. Bake.

    Bake until set in the center and just beginning to crack , rotating the sheets halfway through. They should not brown — that's the tell.

  9. Cool & store.

    Let them sit on the sheet , then transfer to a wire rack. Airtight container, up to a week — though they rarely make it that far.

— from Devon's kitchen

A snickerdoodle by another name. The shortening is non-negotiable; it's what gives the cookie its plush, almost cake-like center while the butter handles the flavor.
